# Why Enterprise AI Pilots Fail

AI pilots often start with a narrow question: can a model produce useful output? That question is easy to answer and usually misleading. The harder question is whether the organization can turn that capability into a repeatable workflow with ownership, permissions, measurement, support, and trust.

A successful pilot needs a path to production from the beginning. That means clear users, clear data boundaries, clear approval paths, and a platform team that understands how the workflow will be operated after the demo.

## The Real Failure Mode

Most pilots do not fail because the model is weak. They fail because the system around the model is missing. Teams need retrieval, context, observability, governance, developer education, and practical patterns for where AI should and should not be used.

Enterprise AI work is platform work. The model matters, but the durable value comes from the scaffolding that makes AI safe, inspectable, and useful inside actual business processes.
