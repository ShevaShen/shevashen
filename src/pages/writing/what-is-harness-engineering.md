# What Is Harness Engineering?

Harness engineering is the work of surrounding a foundation model with the structures it needs to operate usefully: context, tools, instructions, memory, permissions, evaluations, policies, and human control points.

The term matters because it shifts attention away from prompts as isolated text and toward the complete operating environment. In enterprise settings, the harness determines whether an AI system can be trusted with real workflows.

## What the Harness Provides

A good harness gives the model relevant context, limits what it can access, defines what it can do, captures what happened, and routes uncertain cases to people. It also creates a path for improvement through evaluation and observability.

Agent systems should be judged by the behavior of the whole harness, not by a single impressive response.
