# The Real Work Starts After Choosing a Model

Choosing a model is an important decision, but it is not a strategy. A model becomes useful when it is connected to the right context, placed inside a workflow, evaluated against real outcomes, and operated with the same seriousness as any other production capability.

The platform questions arrive immediately. Who is allowed to use which data? Which actions require human approval? How are failures reviewed? How do teams reuse successful patterns without copying fragile prototypes?

## Platform Leverage

Strong AI platforms make the right thing easier to do repeatedly. They provide shared primitives for retrieval, tools, permissions, observability, evaluation, and release management. They also give teams a language for discussing risk and value without turning every project into a one-off negotiation.

The model is the engine. The platform is the vehicle, road, dashboard, and operating manual.
